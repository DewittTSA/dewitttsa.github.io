Tidal Energy

Tidal is energy is a form of power that turns Earth's tides' energy into electricity. Usually, one would install an underwater turbine on the seabed, where there is high tidal velocity. The tides turn the turbine, which generates electricity. The tides themselves are created by the gravitational pull of the moon and the sun. This means that tidal energy is predictable, as well as renewable and Eco-friendly.

Pros and Cons of Tidal Energy

There are both good sides and bad sides of tidal energy, as applies to all forms of energy.

Pros

‣ Perfectly renewable. 
‣ Green! Relatively Environmentally friendly.
‣ Tidal Energy is predictable, as mentioned above.
‣ Able to generate electricity even with low tidal           speeds.
‣ Tidal Energy turbines can last for a long time, 
    making their cost worth it.

Cons

‣ Exploiting tidal energy is expensive.
‣ There are some environmental impacts:
          May reduce kinetic energy in the ocean
          May capture pollution, dirt, etc.
          Impact on fish, birds, marine life
          May disrupt the tidal cycles

How technology is related to Tidal Energy:

Technology can help create better and cheaper ways for workers to install tidal turbines.
With funding, the costs of tidal technology can decrease just like the costs of wind energy tech, and prices to install will drop.
Different innovations in tidal energy technology can reduce the environmental impacts that are currently present.
