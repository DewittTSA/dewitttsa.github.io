Biomass Energy

Biomass is a renewable source of fuel to produce energy because: waste residues will always exist – in terms of scrap wood, mill residuals and forest resources; and, properly managed forests will always have more trees, and we will always have crops and the residual biological matter from those crops.

Biomass Energy

The burning of biological materials. 

What IS Biomass Energy?

Biomass energy is based off of burning biological matter in order to generate energy (in its multiple forms).

Why is Biomass Energy Effective?

Biomass-based energy generation is effective because it is renewable. The items that we turn into energy can grow back extremely quickly compared to the time it takes for fossil fuels to form. We can dispose of excess matter while also generating energy!

How Does Technology Affect Biomass Energy?

As our technology improves, so does biomass-based energy. It will become more effective and efficient. We can improve the effectiveness of the conversions to electricity, resulting in more value and, of course, more electricity.

For example, we can try to shrink the size of the conversion plants, resulting in them taking less space, helping to resolve one of the cons of biomass energy, which is the size of the plants.



Careers Pertaining to Biomass Energy

There are multiple careers that are related to biomass energy. If we further implement biomass energy, careers and jobs such as environmental expert, project administration, service technicians, and much much more will arise.
Pros


‣ Highly renewable, uses abundant resources

‣ Disposes of organic waste

‣ Low carbon emissions, especially compared to that of other energy-generation methods.


Cons

‣ Pollutes the surrounding area, possibly disturbing nearby people

‣ Expensive to create the biomass plants and expensive to maintain the operation. 

‣ Isn't very efficient -- you have to burn extremely large quantities of biomass in order to equal the small burning of another type of fuel.

‣ Has a high number of emissions (other than carbon), although this may depend on what materials you burn. Additionally, fuel is required in order to burn the biological matter.
