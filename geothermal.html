Geothermal Energy

Geothermal energy comes from the heat beneath the Earth's crust, which is both clean and renewable. Geothermal pumps can tap under the Earth's crust to access this heat, which is transferred to geothermal power plants. Some geothermal power plants use the steam from the reservoirs to turn turbines to generate electricity, while others use the heat from the geothermal pumps to boil types of liquid, which would also turn turbines when evaporated.

Pros of Geothermal Energy

​‣ Geothermal Energy is CLEAN and RENEWABLE (Environmentally friendly)
‣ Geothermal Energy could produce massive amounts of energy
‣ Geothermal Energy could also be used to heat and cool homes
‣ Geothermal Energy is pretty cheap (No fuel required)
‣ Geothermal Energy is available everywhere (May be extremely expensive and not profitable in some places)
‣ Technology could help Geothermal Energy become better in general
‣ Small landscape footprint (Built partially or mostly underground)
Cons of Geothermal Energy

‣ There are some minor environmental issues
‣ Reservoirs need to be properly managed
‣ Sites where Geothermal Energy would be profitable are often far from populated areas
‣ Energy would be lost (long distance for the electricity to travel)

How Technology is Incorporated into Geothermal Energy

There are many ways that technology can help geothermal energy advance.

﻿‣ Technology makes drilling into reservoirs cheaper and better
‣ Technology could make transmitting electricity for long distances more efficient
‣ Technology could help make the geothermal pumps more efficient, which makes exploiting this energy source a better option than choosing fossil fuels.
‣ Technology could provide access to deeper areas under the Earth's crust, so the Geothermal Power Plants could be located closer to residential areas
